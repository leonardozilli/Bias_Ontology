Abstract

The anchoring bias occurs when individuals rely too heavily on an initial piece of information, called an anchor, when making judgments or decisions. The anchor could be a number, value, or any relevant information provided at the beginning of a decision-making process. Subsequently, individuals adjust their judgments or decisions around this initial anchor. The bias occurs because people tend to insufficiently adjust their judgments from the initial reference point, resulting in systematic errors.
